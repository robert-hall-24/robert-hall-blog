Blocked On a Small Problem



Javascript is challenging to learn at the start. One of the things we easily miss at the beginning is typos and mistakes with naming objects or arrays and calling them correctly since we are absorbing a huge amount of information.

The one small problem I constantly encounter is forgetting letters from the objects or arrays I create. The biggest culprit is the letter S! The number of terms I have created, objects or arrays, called letters or words, and typing out words or letters when calling them was way too common.

This was very frustrating, especially when you redo your code to be more complex, and the fix is simple.

The main lesson from this is to check that the objects, arrays, and functions you call are spelled correctly before diving into your code and redoing it.
